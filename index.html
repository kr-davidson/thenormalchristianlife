The Normal
Christian Life

Watchman Nee

This study walks through Watchman Nee's foundational work one chapter at a time over thirteen weeks. Each week focuses on a single central truth, with guided reflection questions, Scripture engagement, and prayer. The goal is not to rush through a book, but to allow its truths to take root in daily life. The progression is deliberate: each week builds on the last, and the order matters.

The Christian life begins with what God has done in Christ, not with what we do for God.

The Study Path

Thirteen weeks. Each chapter addresses a distinct dimension of the Christian life. Work through them in sequence. The earlier chapters lay the foundation that makes the later ones intelligible.

The Structure of the Christian Life in This Study

The Normal Christian Life is not a collection of independent topics. It unfolds in three deliberate movements, each building on the last. Understanding the sequence prevents a great deal of confusion. Many believers have struggled unnecessarily because they tried to enter a later movement without having settled the foundation of an earlier one.

Movement Focus What It Addresses Weeks
The Blood Forgiveness of sins The guilt of specific wrongs committed before a holy God. Deals with our record. Week 1
The Cross Deliverance from sin The power of indwelling sin and the old nature. Deals with our condition through union with Christ in His death and resurrection. Weeks 2–6
The Spirit and the Body Living the Christian life Walking in the Spirit, overcoming inward conflict, corporate life in Christ, the soul life, and ongoing surrender. Deals with our daily walk. Weeks 7–13
Why the Order Matters

The Christian life is not random or fragmented. Each movement builds on the one before it. The blood must be understood before the cross can be received. The cross must do its work before the Spirit can flow freely. And the life of the Spirit must be established before the corporate and deeper dimensions of the final weeks make sense. Misunderstanding the order leads to confusion. Following it leads to clarity.

How to Use This Study

This study is designed for personal use, though it works well in small groups as well. Move at a pace that allows the material to be absorbed, not merely read.

  • 1
    One chapter per week. Read each chapter of the book slowly before engaging with the study page. The study page is a companion, not a replacement for reading Nee directly.
  • 2
    Engage the reflection questions honestly. These questions are not designed to be answered quickly. Some may take a day to sit with. Consider working through them in a personal journal.
  • 3
    Look up the Scripture references. Each passage is provided in context. Allow the biblical text to carry the weight, not merely the summary on the page.
  • 4
    Pray before and after each session. Each chapter page ends with a written prayer. Use it as a starting point, not a script. The goal is honest engagement with God, not the completion of an exercise.
  • 5
    Progress gradually. Each chapter depends on the one before it. Nee's argument builds deliberately. The discipline of following the sequence is itself part of the formation.

About This Book

The Normal Christian Life by Watchman Nee is one of the most enduring works of twentieth-century Christian literature. Originally delivered as a series of addresses in Europe in the 1930s, the book presents the Christian life not as an achievement of spiritual maturity, but as the natural outworking of understanding what God has already accomplished in Christ.

Nee writes with pastoral clarity and theological precision. His central argument is that the Christian life, in its normal form, is characterised by rest rather than effort, by faith rather than feeling, and by what God has done rather than what the believer must do. This study is designed to help readers encounter those truths slowly and carefully, in the order Nee intended.
